Blake with Elbert and a Cathouse Rescued from the Alley

Blake with one of his two cats, Elbert. Elbert is the friendly one. Prominently featured in this picture is a gigantic cat house that somebody threw out. Blake rescued it, and is recarpeting it. Elbert and the other, less-friendly cat seem to really like it.

Playing a Game About Dogs in a Football Stadium

This is actually pretty cool. The game was fun, but the best part was we were playing it, on a freaking football field. Lucas Oil Stadium, if you must know. Gencon started using the stadium as part of their event space a couple of years ago. Pretty neat to get to walk around on it and do non-football-related-activities.

Possibly a Mint Julep But Probably Not

Prototypical Blake

Fun fact: at Gencon, you can play prototypes of games that haven't been published yet. This was one such game. That guy who introduced me to Blake makes a point of signing us up for at least one prototype play every year.

Three Blakes

Three Blakes ended up randomly playing a game together! The world doesn't know how close it came to being swallowed up by some kind of giant Universal Coincidence that day. And it must never know.

The shocking truth about Blake!

Blake's a friend of mine; he works for LaunchCode; he encouraged me to take this class. He's a great guy, one of several exceptional friends I am lucky to have found here in St. Louis, including Jeremy (not me) who is the 'other guy' pictured several times above. Jeremy also happens to be a programmer of note and has also been very encouraging as I contemplate changing careers. Chuck, not pictured, is also a friend, also a programmer, and it was his basement that was featured in the basement paragraph above. And I'm not stalking Blake, I just take lots of pictures every year when we go to Gencon together and I pretty much never delete any of them from my phone.
